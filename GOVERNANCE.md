# Governance Model



## 1. Steering Committee



The OGRE Team Steering Committee (SC) is responsible for high-level direction, scope control, and standards enforcement across OpenGovCon repositories.



The SC has final authority over:

- Roadmap direction

- Licensing and trademark policy

- Compliance standards

- Maintainer approvals



## 2. Maintainers



Each repository has designated maintainers responsible for:

- reviewing pull requests

- enforcing code standards

- maintaining test and evidence integrity

- managing releases



Maintainers must follow Steering Committee standards.



## 3. Decision Process



Decisions are made via:

- GitHub issues + discussion

- recorded votes in the repo under `/decisions/`



Voting model:

- Majority vote for standard decisions

- Supermajority required for license changes



## 4. Conflict of Interest



Any participant representing a commercial vendor must disclose conflicts of interest.



The SC may restrict voting rights for individuals with direct competitive conflicts.



## 5. Removal



Maintainers or SC members may be removed by majority vote for:

- misconduct

- repeated violations of contribution rules

- hostile or bad-faith participation
